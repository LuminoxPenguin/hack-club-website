10 Fun Facts about 3 Differnet Types of Peguins

Emperor Penguin

1. Wild Emperor penguins are only found in Antarctica.

2. Emperor Penguins are the biggest modern penguin species, and one of the largest of all birds. They are approximately 120cm tall.

3. Emperors are the least common penguin in Antarctica.

4. Emperor penguin colonies have been discovered and counted from space.

5. During egg incubation, The male carefully balances the egg on his feet for between 65 and 75 days to keep it warm in a specially adapted brood pouch

6. When Antarctica gets really cold, 5,000 or more emperor penguin adults and chicks huddle for warmth

7. The deepest dive an emperor penguin has performed is 564 m which is 1850 ft

8. Emperor penguins eat anywhere from 2-3 kg per day normally up to 4-6kg to store up body fat for winter or feeding

9. Male emperor penguins during incubation may not eat for up to four months until the egg hatches

10. Emperor penguins are able to climb steep ice cliffs

Adélie Penguin

1. Adélie Penguins were discovered only in 1840

2. Adélie Penguins are the smallest species of penguin in Antarctica

3. Adélie Penguins often attack predators or researchers with their flippers

4. Adélie Penguins when making their nest may steal from othe Adélie Penguins to try and make the biggest nest

5. Adélie Penguins are unable to eat for 3 weeks a year after they moult

6. Adélie Penguins typically swim at around 2.5 - 5 mph, and can reach up to 9.3mph when hunting or fleeing while swimming

7. Only the Adélie Penguin and Emperor Penguin live and breed in Antarctica

8. Adélie Penguins can dive up to 590 ft

9. Adélie Penguins are often compared to tuxedos in their look as they have a white front and are completely black on the back and head

10. Adélie Penguins leave their colony to go to sea at only 7 to 9 weeks old

Little Blue Penguin

1. Little Blue Penguins are also known as Fairy Penguins

2. Little Blue Penguins live in New Zealand, Southern Australia, and some usrrounding islands

3. Little Blue Penguins are the smallest penguin species only growing to 16 inches

4. Little Blue Penguins are able to sleep while floating in water

5. Most Little Blue Penguins live in the same burrow for their entire lives

6. Little Blue Penguins have swam at a maximum recored speed of 4 mph

7. Little Blue Penguins go almost a mile inland to find the perfect nest site

8. There are around 470 thousand Little Blue Penguins

9. Little Blue Penguins live for around 7 years

10. Little Blue Penguins often leave their burrow before sunrise and return after sunset
